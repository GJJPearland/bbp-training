Training Overview

This training covers OSHA's Bloodborne Pathogen Standard as it applies to our BJJ school environment

  • Who: All staff members who may encounter blood during their duties
  • Frequency: Required annually
  • Standard: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1030
  • Purpose: Protect workers from health hazards related to bloodborne pathogens

What Are Bloodborne Pathogens?

Infectious microorganisms found in human blood that can cause serious diseases

Hepatitis B (HBV)

Attacks the liver

Hepatitis C (HCV)

Attacks the liver

HIV

Attacks the immune system

Transmission: Through contact with infected blood or certain body fluids

Occupational Exposure in Our BJJ School

You have occupational exposure if your job duties may involve contact with:

  • Blood from cuts, bloody noses, or busted lips
  • Blood on training mats or equipment
  • Contaminated cleaning materials
  • Providing first aid to injured students

Common Scenarios:

  • Student gets a cut during training
  • Bloody nose during sparring
  • Busted lip from contact
  • Blood drops on mats that need cleaning

Universal Precautions

GOLDEN RULE

Treat ALL blood and body fluids as if they are infectious

This means:

  • Always use protective equipment when handling blood
  • Never assume blood is "safe" based on who it came from
  • Follow the same safety procedures every time
  • Don't take shortcuts, even for small amounts of blood

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Required PPE for Blood Cleanup:

Disposable gloves (nitrile or latex)

PPE Guidelines:

  • Put on gloves BEFORE touching anything contaminated
  • Remove gloves properly (don't touch outside surface)
  • Wash hands immediately after removing gloves
  • Replace damaged or contaminated PPE immediately
  • Never reuse disposable gloves

Blood Cleanup Procedures (Steps 1-3)

1. Secure the Area

Place cones or barriers around contaminated area to keep students away

2. Put on Gloves

Before touching anything contaminated

3. Clean Up Visible Blood

Using disposable towels

Blood Cleanup Procedures (Steps 4-6)

4. Apply Mat Attack Disinfectant

Liberally cover the entire contaminated area

5. Let Disinfectant Sit for 10 Minutes Minimum

  • Surface must remain visibly wet during entire 10-minute period
  • Ensures effectiveness against HIV (2 min), Hepatitis B (10 min), and Hepatitis C (10 min)
  • Reapply if surface begins to dry

6. Dispose of Contaminated Materials

In regular trash (in sealed plastic bag)

Blood Cleanup Procedures (Steps 7-9)

7. Remove Gloves Properly

And dispose of them safely

8. Wash Hands Thoroughly

With soap and warm water

9. Remove Barriers

Only after full 10-minute contact time

Remember: Classes may continue in unaffected areas while cleanup is in progress

First Aid Procedures

When Providing First Aid:

  • Always wear gloves before touching injured person
  • Minimize contact with blood and body fluids
  • Use barrier devices for mouth-to-mouth resuscitation
  • Handle contaminated items carefully
  • Clean up properly after providing aid

For Serious Injuries:

  • Call 911 if needed
  • Apply direct pressure to control bleeding (with gloves)
  • Elevate injured area if appropriate
  • Stay calm and keep student comfortable
  • Follow up with incident documentation

If You Are Exposed to Blood

1. Immediate Action:

  • Wash exposed skin with soap and warm water
  • Flush eyes, nose, or mouth with clean water
  • Report to management immediately

2. Medical Evaluation:

  • Seek medical attention promptly
  • We will provide medical evaluation at no cost
  • Follow all medical recommendations

3. Documentation:

  • Complete incident report
  • Maintain confidential medical records

Contaminated Laundry Protocol

All Gis with Blood (Instructors and Students):

  • Anyone with blood on their gi takes it home to wash
  • Do NOT send blood-contaminated gis to outsourced laundry service
  • Only non-contaminated uniforms go to laundry service
  • Recommend washing bloody gis in hot water with bleach at home

Other Contaminated Materials:

  • Bloody paper towels/cleaning materials: Regular trash in sealed plastic bag
  • Large blood spills: May require professional cleanup/disposal
  • Student gis: Students responsible for their own contaminated gear

Hepatitis B Vaccination

Employer Responsibilities:

  • Offer vaccination to all at-risk employees at no cost
  • Provide within 10 working days of assignment
  • Use licensed healthcare professional

Employee Rights:

  • You may accept or decline vaccination
  • If declined, you must sign a declination form
  • You can request vaccination later if you change your mind
  • No cost to you for vaccination or evaluation
About the Vaccine: Safe and effective protection given as 3 shots over 6 months

Engineering and Work Practice Controls

Engineering Controls:

  • Handwashing stations readily available
  • First aid supplies easily accessible
  • Proper lighting for cleanup activities
  • Non-slip surfaces to prevent injuries

Work Practice Controls:

  • No eating, drinking, or smoking in areas where blood exposure may occur
  • Proper hand hygiene after glove removal
  • Immediate cleanup of blood spills

Training Requirements

Initial Training:

All new employees before starting duties

Annual Training:

  • Required every 12 months
  • Updates on new procedures or equipment
  • Refresher on all key concepts

Additional Training:

  • When job duties change
  • When new equipment is introduced
  • After exposure incidents if needed

Emergency Post-Exposure Protocol

Follow these steps immediately:

  1. Immediately wash exposed area with soap and warm water
  2. Report to management immediately (806-544-9557)
  3. Seek immediate medical evaluation (call 911 or go to nearest ER)
  4. Contact employee's personal healthcare provider
  5. Complete incident documentation
  6. Follow up as directed by medical professional

Key Takeaways

Remember These Critical Points:

  1. Universal Precautions: Treat all blood as infectious
  2. PPE is Required: Always wear gloves when handling blood
  3. Proper Cleanup: Follow step-by-step procedures every time
  4. Report Exposures: Immediate reporting and medical evaluation
  5. Annual Training: Required every year to stay current
